a(nother) machine

there is a misconception that in order to exist, good things on the net must require an exchange of personal data, manufactured scarcity, and a profit motive.

somewhere along the way we forgot how simple the web can actually be.

at times we looked down at our feet, caught between a stronger password and a desktop notification.

in other moments, we found ourselves heads tipped back, yearning for something better than what people told us the web had to be.

passing captchas, installing ad blockers, agreeing to cookie policies, and ejecting out of scroll blocking email forms.

many experiences and the tools that build them exist to further this idea of the web.

we make experiences and tools to build them with a better web in mind.

we distribute tools via ampl, the another machine public library.

Devices
motion, orientation, user media, and midi

Lexicon
topics and constraints for word based games

Music Detection
detecting music in audio streams

Music Theory
music notation & key generation

Procedural Generation
rng device synchronization

Sound Synthesis
web oscillators

Steganography
encoding and decoding data in image pixels

we also love to use these tools to make compelling experiences, free from cloud complexity, cheap to distribute, free to use.

AVVA
two independent machines, one turns music into color, the other color into music. point them at each other, then get in-between.

Lexicon Standoff
a three+ player word game, try to determine who is an imposter

Mindmeld
quick two-person collaborative word game for idle time

Sonic Pixels
a sound player, where digital images are the file format

we are extremely proud of how light and tidy these resources are. the goal of ampl is to make a suite of tools that embrace limitation to make more creative web experiments. the goal of our machines is to demonstrate alternative decentralized uses of web technology.
