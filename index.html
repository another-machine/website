a(nother) machine

There is a misconception that in order to exist, good things on the net must require an exchange of personal data, manufactured scarcity, and a profit motive.

At some point we forgot how simple the web can be.

At times we looked down, thinking of a stronger password, ignoring an unwanted desktop notification.

In other moments, our heads tipped back, yearning for something better than what people told us the web had to be.

We fail captchas, install ad blockers, block location access requests, agree to cookie policies, and frantically close scroll locking popups.

Many experiences and the tools that build them exist to further this abrasive form of the web.

We make experiences and tools to build them with an expressive web in mind.

We distribute our tools via the Another Machine Public Library.

...or ampl for short.

Devices
Motion, UserMedia, MIDI, and Orientation tools.

Lexicon
Topics and constraints for word based games.

Music Detection
Detecting music in audio streams.

Music Theory
Music notation and chromatic scale generation.

Procedural Generation
RNG device synchronization.

Sound Synthesis
Web oscillators.

Steganography
Encoding and decoding data in image pixels.

We use these tools to make compelling experiences, free from cloud complexity, cheap to distribute, and free to use.

AVVA
Two independent machines, one turns music into color, the other color into music. Point them at each other, and then get in-between them.

Lexicon Standoff
A party word game of hidden identities.

Mindmeld
A two-person collaborative word game for idle time.

Sonic Pixels
A sound player using digital images as the file format.

a(nother) machine is here to make tools that work within constraints to make a more expressive web. Our experiences demonstrate alternative uses of web technology. Our hope is all of this makes you excited to build something similar.
